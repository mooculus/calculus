\begin{exercise}

  You have $100$ feet of fence to make a rectangular play area
  alongside the wall of your house.  The wall of the house bounds one
  side.  What is the largest possible area (in square feet)?

\begin{hint}
We have to maximize the area, $A$.
Since, $100=2x+y$, it follows that

$y=100-2x$.
\end{hint}
\begin{hint}
We now express the area as the function of $x$.

$A(x)=\answer{x}\cdot(100-2x)$

So, we have to find the global maximum of the function $A$, given by

$A(x)=100\cdot\answer{x}-2x^2$

on its domain $[0,50]$.
\end{hint}
\begin{hint}
We have to find all the critical points of $A$ on $[0,50]$.
Since

$A'(x)=\answer{100}-4x$,

it follows that  the function $A$ has its only critical point at $x=\answer{25}$.

We have to evaluate $A$ at the end points and the critical point.

$A(0)=\answer{0}$

$A(50)=\answer{0}$

$A(25)=\answer{1250}$

Now we compare these three values, and determine the global maximum of $A$.
\end{hint}
