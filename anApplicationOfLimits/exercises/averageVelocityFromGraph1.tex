\begin{exercise}

An object is moving along a horizontal line.  Let $s(t)$ be the position of the object (in meters) at time $t$ (in seconds).  The graph of $s(t)$ is given below.

\begin{image}
  \begin{tikzpicture}
    \begin{axis}[
        xmin=-.3,xmax=4.3,ymin=-1.3,ymax=1.3,
        clip=true,
        unit vector ratio*=1 1 1,
        axis lines=center,
        grid = major,
        ytick={-1,0,...,1},
    xtick={0,1,...,4},
        xlabel=$t$, ylabel=$y$,
        every axis y label/.style={at=(current axis.above origin),anchor=south},
        every axis x label/.style={at=(current axis.right of origin),anchor=west},
      ]
      \addplot[very thick,penColor,domain=0:2,samples=50] plot{-(x-1)^3};
      \addplot[very thick,penColor,domain=2:4,samples=50] plot{-(x-4)^2/4};
      
      \node at (axis cs:1.5,0.7) [penColor] {$y=s(t)$};
      \end{axis}`
  \end{tikzpicture}
\end{image}
Fill in the blanks.
\[
s(0) = \answer{1}.
\]

the object is at the origin at $t = \answer{1}$ and $t = \answer{4}$.

The object is furthest from the origin at $t = \answer{0}$ and $t = \answer{2}$.

The average velocity of the object on the interval $[0,2]$ is
\[
v_{\text{av}} = \answer{-2}\text{m/s.}
\]

\end{exercise}
